SPS Commerce

This is one of those “What do you get the friend that has everything?” kind of stories. In this scenario, you give them the opportunity to come up with ideas, and then validate them with others.

Tools/Methodologies Used: Design Thinking Workshops, User Research, Miro, Balsamiq, Sketch, InVision

Specializing in retailer relationship management and demand and supply planning, SPS Commerce already had a lot to offer. With that in mind, we got together to identify, prioritize, and validate several add-on ideas for their product, Fulfillment.

We began with a two-day Design Thinking Workshop that allowed us to gather information such as customer pain points and needs, in turn helping our team and the client to build empathy.

Ecosystem Map built out after our initial Design Thinking workshop.

Next, we put on our traveling gear, and ventured out to visit a few of SPS Commerce's customers about how and why they use Fulfillment.

This in-person user research proved incredibly useful, as we found out that some customers were a bit more willing to express real issues and pain points with a third party group. While it can be tough to hear, it provided us with opportunities as well.

A handful of warehouses and colorful interactions later, our time spent with real customers had given us enough to bring back to the mother ship and begin refining some ideas.

We regrouped with the team at SPS Commerce and began running through user scenarios and validating the lo-fidelity prototypes of our add-on ideas.

One of the many lo-fidelity prototypes built in Balsamiq.

After some real talk and refining, we narrowed our add-ons down and began work on our hi-fidelity prototypes that could be presented to customers, get validated as valuable, and then make a decision on moving forward with a build phase.

Hi-fidelity prototypes built in Sketch and InVision.

In the end, we presented SPS Commerce with all our findings, and talked about the business viability of moving forward with the add-on ideas that made it through the gamut.

Just as important, we were able help them build better relationships with their clients, which will in turn allow them to better understand and serve them.
